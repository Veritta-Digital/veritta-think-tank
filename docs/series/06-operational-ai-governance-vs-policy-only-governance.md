# Operational AI Governance vs Policy-Only Governance

Policy matters.
But policy by itself does not govern a live system.

A system becomes operationally governed when constraints, checkpoints, evidence and responsibility are embedded into the way work is actually executed.

## Policy-only governance tends to say
- what should happen;
- what should not happen;
- which values matter.

## Operational governance must also answer
- what blocks execution;
- who approves progression;
- what counts as evidence;
- where canonical precedence lives;
- how drift is detected;
- how rollback and remediation occur.

This distinction is decisive.
A team may have beautifully written AI principles and still run systems that expand scope silently, hide debt, lose traceability and blur accountability.

Operational AI governance begins where slogans stop and engineering, documentation and decision architecture start working together.
