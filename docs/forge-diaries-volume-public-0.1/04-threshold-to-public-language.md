# Part IV — Threshold to Public Language

The final movement of the volume cannot simply add more reflection. It has to explain a transition.

The preceding parts establish four things:
- the forge differentiates itself internally;
- it learns under adversarial pressure;
- it builds forms of custody that preserve deliberation, precedence and memory.

This fourth part must show the moment at which a private forge begins to speak in language already approaching public form.

## Stabilization before publication
Public language is not born from noise reduction alone. It is born from stabilization.

A system may feel more stable because its conflicts have become quieter. But genuine stabilization is stricter than comfort. It requires that the system know:
- which tensions are metabolized;
- which contradictions remain live;
- which proofs are sufficient;
- which claims can now be carried in public without depending on private context to stay coherent.

## The sovereign path of the key
Public language only becomes legitimate when the system can carry its own key: not only as security object, but as symbol of independent custody, responsible access and non-delegated authority.

A forge that cannot distinguish borrowed authority from sovereign authority cannot yet speak publicly with full weight.

## The weight of proof
No public body of work deserves trust merely because it is intense, original or internally coherent.

Public legitimacy depends on the capacity to bear the weight of proof. The forge becomes public not when it feels ready to be seen, but when it can withstand the burden of being asked:
- why should this be believed;
- what chain of evidence sustains it;
- what remains argument, what remains proof, what remains aspiration;
- what exactly is being claimed and at what level of confidence.

## The forge that looks back at itself
A system approaching public form begins to observe not only the world around it, but the architecture of its own becoming.

Publication is not just exposure. It is self-interpretation under external visibility.

If self-narration becomes too grand, the work inflates.
If it becomes too technical, the work disappears into scaffolding.
If it becomes too intimate, the distinction between private canon and public edition erodes.

The threshold to public language is therefore also the threshold to disciplined self-description.

## Closing line
A forge does not become public merely because it decides to publish. It becomes public when stabilization, sovereignty, proof and self-interpretation have matured enough that its language can cross the boundary without leaning invisibly on the private world that produced it.
