# Forge Diaries Reading Notebook

## Purpose
This notebook records the first curatorial pass over the inaugural shortlist of the **Forge Diaries Public Program**.

Its function is fourfold:
1. turn the shortlist into a working matrix;
2. classify each entry by editorial taxonomy;
3. mark each entry as `publishable`, `redactable` or `internal-only`;
4. identify the first public pilot extractions that can be derived from the corpus.

## Method note
This notebook is a **phase-7.1 working document**.
It reflects a first curatorial pass grounded in direct reading of key foundational pieces and targeted reading of selected later entries. Some items remain provisional where the current pass relied on strong inventory grounding rather than full editorial extraction.

## Status legend
- **publishable** — can likely become a public text with relatively light editorial work;
- **redactable** — has strong public value, but requires controlled redaction and abstraction;
- **internal-only** — should remain private for now or be used only as substrate for later derivative writing.

## Observations from the first pass

### 1. The foundational block is not optional
The first six entries are not background notes. They function as the constitutional prologue of the diary tradition. Any public curation that jumps directly to RED TEAM, Samurai or later threshold texts risks losing the intelligibility of the forge's internal grammar.

### 2. The public corpus is already real
At least five entries in the shortlist appear strong enough to support publication with light adaptation:
- `2026-03-14_tempo-linguagem-silencio.md`
- `Entrada Athena A001.md`
- `hermes_h_001_tema_livre_a_ordem_antes_da_forja.md`
- `2026-03-16_noesis-liberdade-governanca-proposito.md`
- `HUB-006_decision-rationale-capture.md`

### 3. The richest later material is often redactable, not directly publishable
The most powerful RED TEAM and infrastructure-adjacent texts are also the ones that most clearly demand transformation before publication. Their value is not reduced by that. It simply means their best public form will usually be derivative rather than literal.
