# Forge Diaries Public Program

## Purpose
This document establishes the public editorial program for the future publication of selected writings derived from the **Diários da Forja** corpus.

The purpose of the program is not to publish raw diaries indiscriminately.
Its purpose is to create a governed pathway for translating a private, historically rich and operationally sensitive corpus into a public editorial layer.

## Governing rule
**RAW != PUBLICÁVEL**

The private canon may be read in full for curatorial purposes, including archived and consolidated material. Public publication, however, must always occur through selection, redaction, abstraction and editorial stabilization.

## Why the program exists
The known corpus already contains a large and coherent body of work, including dozens of diary entries, TEMA LIVRE compositions, executive orders and adversarial reflections. It is therefore mature enough to justify a public program of reading and preparation.

At the same time, the corpus contains operational sensitivity, internal vocabulary, structural detail and private historical density that make raw publication inappropriate.

This program exists to govern that translation.

## Method — chronological reading before editorial extraction
The reading program begins chronologically and includes archived material whenever archived originals are richer than later consolidated versions.

The order is deliberate. A public volume should not begin with mature later-stage reflections while omitting the foundational texts that explain how the forge learned to speak in the first place.

## Foundational mandatory prologue
Before the program moves into RED TEAM, SAMURAI or later public-facing threshold texts, it must read and classify the foundational block below.

### Foundational block
1. **ANUBIS 001** — `2026-03-14_forja-tripla-e-despertar-dos-subagentes.md`
2. **ANUBIS 002** — `2026-03-14_tema-livre-quem-e-anubis.md`
3. **ANUBIS 003** — `2026-03-14_tempo-linguagem-silencio.md`
4. **ATHENA A001** — `Entrada Athena A001.md`
5. **HERMES H001** — `hermes_h_001_tema_livre_a_ordem_antes_da_forja.md`
6. **HORUS-001** — `HORUS-001_o-olho-que-herdou-tres-naus.md`

### Why this block is mandatory
These texts establish the four structural axes without which the later corpus becomes harder to interpret publicly:
- ANUBIS: execution, jurimetric discipline, identity, time and silence;
- ATHENA: interface, cognitive containment and protection of the human operator;
- HERMES: order, sequence, jurisdiction and architectural ethics;
- HORUS: infrastructure, fleet reality and the material body of the forge.

They are not marginal context. They are the constitutional prologue of the diary tradition.
