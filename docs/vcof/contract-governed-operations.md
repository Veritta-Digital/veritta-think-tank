# Contract-Governed Operations in V-COF

## Purpose
This note explains the procedural layer of V-COF that operates through contracts, gates, seals and bounded agent interactions.

## Core idea
The framework is broader than a single execution ritual, but many of its concrete operations share a contract-governed structure:
- a defined mandate;
- a bounded scope;
- explicit constraints;
- checkpoints before progression;
- evidence on completion;
- human authority over consequential closure.

## Why this matters
Without a contract-governed layer, agentic systems tend to expand beyond their declared mandate. They interpret missing context too freely, collapse distinctions too early and continue operating after the human operator has lost meaningful control.

## Relation to AGC
An AGC can be read as a micro-contractual envelope around an agent call. It specifies:
- which agent is invoked;
- under what scope;
- with which constraints;
- toward what expected output;
- under what failure condition work must stop.

## Relation to gates and seals
Contracts define the frame of work.
Gates regulate progression.
Seals document verifiable closure.
Together, they produce a workflow in which useful output is not enough: process integrity must also be preserved.

## Naming note
This contractual layer should not be confused with the official expansion of the acronym V-COF.
The canonical public name remains **Verittà Cognitive-Operational Framework**.
Contract-governed operations are better understood as one of its central procedural expressions.
