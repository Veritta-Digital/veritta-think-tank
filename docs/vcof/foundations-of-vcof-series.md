# Foundations of V-COF — Companion Public Series

## Purpose
This note defines the first outward-facing companion series derived from the existing public V-COF materials.

The series is not meant to replace the constitutional note, the governance principles or the Forge Diaries volume.
Its purpose is to make the framework easier to approach through a short, didactic and sequential public pathway.

## Why this series should exist now
The repository already has:
- a public constitutional note;
- public governance principles;
- a note on contract-governed operations;
- essays on operational governance, fail-closed, debt governance, decision rationale and freedom under governance.

What is still missing is a compact sequence that allows an external reader to move from first contact to conceptual fluency without having to reconstruct the framework from scattered notes.

## Format
Initial wave: **6 short public pieces**.

These pieces should be:
- short enough to be independently readable;
- strong enough to carry institutional seriousness;
- sequential enough to form a guided path;
- bounded enough to avoid turning the framework into slogan language.

## Relationship to the Forge Diaries volume
This series should operate as an outward didactic companion to the volume rather than as a summary of it.

- The volume preserves lineage, structure and editorial density.
- The series provides a more accessible guided path into the same constitutional field.

The series and the volume should therefore reinforce one another without collapsing into duplication.

## Editorial rule
These pieces should not be written as marketing copy.
They should read as compact institutional notes: sober, intelligible, disciplined and publishable.

## Suggested release logic
1. Draft all six pieces.
2. Sequence them as a guided reading path.
3. Release in controlled outward rhythm rather than all at once.
4. Reuse them later as companion onboarding texts for researchers, partners and institutional readers.
