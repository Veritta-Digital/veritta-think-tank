# Public Governance Principles

The Verittà Think Tank presents the following principles as public-facing foundations of the V-COF worldview.

## 1. Human sovereignty
AI may assist, draft, inspect, simulate and organize.
Responsibility, approval and final judgment remain human.

## 2. Fail-closed over silent continuation
When critical conditions are missing, the system should stop and declare the block rather than improvise continuity.

## 3. Evidence before confidence
Claims of operational soundness should be grounded in evidence, not rhetorical confidence.

## 4. Explicit scope
A system is more governable when the boundaries of the task are named rather than silently stretched.

## 5. Canonical precedence
Conflicts between documents, instructions or interpretations require designated precedence.
Without source-of-truth discipline, drift becomes institutional.

## 6. Legible accountability
Useful outputs are insufficient if no one can explain who approved progression, on what basis and with what evidence.

## 7. Curated continuity
Memory should be preserved with hierarchy and custody.
Preservation without curation becomes accumulation; deletion without trail becomes institutional amnesia.
